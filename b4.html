What will be the new trends for MFW? Advances and program

There are a few days left for the start of Milan Fashion Week, and the time has come to find out what will be the new trends proposed by the catwalks for the spring-summer 2019 collections. Neutral colors could play a prominent role: blush, beige, white and black will be highlighted from animalier leathers and fringes. Attention, then, to the boom of the arty chic style, with creations with very refined, if not sophisticated details. The harmony of the contrasts will be visible in the reinterpretation of the couture of the last century in a contemporary perspective, with inlays, patchwork and embroidery ready to arouse everyone's curiosity. Emporio Armani should instead focus on a mood that is both refined and relaxed, such as to combine elegance and class in everyone's personal style: the watchword is " effortless“, Declined in accessories (blazers but not only) that make versatility their strong point, trendy and evergreen.

Program and events

The next Milan Fashion Week will be staged between 19 and 24 September . The actual inauguration, however, will take place on September 18, with special events that will be the prologue to the 61 shows that make up the calendar, juicy anticipation of next year's spring-summer trends. On the catwalk there will be the winner of the “Franca Sozzani GCC Award for Best Emerging Designer”, Tiziano Guardini, who will make his debut on September 20 during the first edition of the Green Carpet Fashion Awards. Among the beginnings to mark on the agenda also those of Fila and AF Vandervorst, but that's not all: the support offered by the National Chamber of Italian Fashion, in fact, will allow the debut of Chika Kisada and Ultrachic, to which the winner of Who's will be added. on Next? 2017, + ACT N.1. There are also returns: on 19 September, for example, it will be the turn of Byblos, followed two days later by Iceberg. As always, the most anticipated big names will play a leading role on the Milan catwalk: on 19 September it will be the turn of Moncler and Alberta Ferretti,Love Moschino , Prada and Fendi. Versace will be on the catwalk on the 21st, and the following day it will be the turn of Agnona, Roberto Cavalli and Salvatore Ferragamo. Grand finale on the 23rd with Giorgio Armani. In all, there will be 9 co-ed shows: in addition to Fila and Emporio Armani, also Tiziano Guardini, Antonio Marras, Salvatore Ferragamo, Moncler , Jil Sandder, GCDS and Byblos. In the Milan Fashion Week calendar there will also be a place for Gucci, which will also be showing in Paris on September 24th, through two cultural events. Finally, on 23 September, the second edition of the Green Carpet Fashion Awards Italia will take place at the Teatro alla Scala, organized with the support of the Municipality of Milan and the Ministry of Economic Development.
